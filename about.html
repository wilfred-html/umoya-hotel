About Umoya

Our Story

The Beginning

A heart stolen by the Cape

Umoya is the brainchild of Georgie Friederichs, a Dutch national whose heart was stolen by the Cape when she first visited in 1994.

Since then, Georgie and her family have travelled extensively throughout the African continent — spending time in Zimbabwe, Botswana, Tanzania, and Namibia. For the last two and a half years they have called Hout Bay home, and even extended their family with the addition of Tamba and Winston, both much-loved fur kids.

The Vision

An authentic experience

Building Umoya has been a labour of love. Georgie and her partners were clear in their vision: to create a space that would provide visitors to the Cape an authentic experience.

The warm terra cotta colours used throughout harmonise with the surrounding environment, infusing a sense of groundedness and natural beauty into every space. Every material was sourced locally, reflecting a deep commitment to community and craft.

Sustainability

Built with the earth in mind

Renewable Energy

Off-grid solar panels power the property, reducing our carbon footprint while keeping your stay comfortable.

Water Conservation

Rainwater catchment systems and a borehole ensure responsible water use in the water-conscious Western Cape.

Indigenous Gardens

Native plants attract bees, birdlife, and butterflies. Our vegetable garden and fruit trees feed our breakfast table.
