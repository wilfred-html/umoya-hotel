Our Story

A love letter to Africa,
written in stone and breath

The Beginning

A Journey Across Africa

In 1994, Georgie Friederichs, a Dutch national with an adventurous spirit, first set foot in Cape Town. What was meant to be a brief visit became a lifelong love affair with the African continent.

Over the years, Georgie traveled extensively throughout Africa — from the wild landscapes of Zimbabwe and Botswana to the vast plains of Tanzania and the stark beauty of Namibia. Each journey deepened her connection to the land, its people, and its extraordinary spirit.

But it was Cape Town that called her home. Specifically, the hidden gem of Hout Bay, nestled between mountains and sea, where the air feels different — cleaner, lighter, more alive. It was here that Georgie decided to create something special.

The Vision

More Than a Hotel

Umoya — meaning "air and breath" in Zulu and Xhosa — was born from Georgie's vision to create an authentic Cape Town experience. Not the sanitized, tourist-brochure version, but the real thing: warm, textured, alive with local culture and natural beauty.

Every decision at Umoya reflects this philosophy. Local artists were commissioned to create unique works for each room. Indigenous gardens were planted to honor the land's natural heritage. Sustainable practices were woven into every operation, from solar panels to rainwater harvesting.

This isn't just hospitality — it's an invitation to experience Cape Town as Georgie does: as a place where nature, art, community, and comfort converge into something greater than the sum of its parts.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Sustainability

We honor the land that hosts us. Our solar panels generate clean energy, rainwater catchment systems reduce water usage, and a borehole ensures water security. Indigenous gardens support local ecosystems, and locally sourced materials minimize our carbon footprint.

Community

Umoya celebrates South African artistry and craftsmanship. Every room features work by local artists. Our ingredients come from nearby farms. Our staff are part of the Hout Bay community. When you stay with us, you support the broader ecosystem that makes this place special.

Authenticity

We don't believe in cookie-cutter luxury. Each room is different. Each day unfolds organically. We welcome you as a guest in our home, not a transaction in a hotel. Real connections, real experiences, real Cape Town.

Mindfulness

Umoya is designed to help you slow down and breathe. From the spacious rooms to the meditation-worthy pool views, every element invites presence. We believe luxury isn't about excess — it's about creating space for what truly matters.

Accessibility

Beautiful experiences should be available to everyone. We offer wheelchair-accessible rooms with thoughtful design that never compromises on aesthetics or comfort. Dignity and luxury aren't mutually exclusive.

Excellence

We obsess over details you might never notice — the thread count of the linens, the pressure of the rain shower, the quality of the coffee beans. Excellence isn't a goal; it's a practice we commit to every single day.

The Umoya Family

Meet Tamba & Winston

No story of Umoya would be complete without mentioning our resident ambassadors: Tamba and Winston, Georgie's beloved dogs who consider themselves the unofficial welcoming committee.

Tamba, the gentle giant, and Winston, the charming rogue, have a sixth sense for when guests need a friendly snout to pet or a warm presence during breakfast on the terrace. They embody the spirit of Umoya — warm, approachable, and impossible not to love.

If you're a dog lover, you're in for a treat. If you're allergic or prefer distance, don't worry — they're well-trained and respectful of boundaries. Just know that their tail wags are legendary, and their affection is genuine.

Sustainability in Action

Energy & Water

Our extensive solar panel installation powers most of our operations with clean energy. Rainwater catchment systems collect and store water for gardens and non-potable use. Our borehole provides a sustainable water source, reducing dependence on municipal supply during Cape Town's dry seasons.

Gardens & Biodiversity

Every plant in our gardens is indigenous to the Cape region. This isn't just aesthetically beautiful — it supports local pollinators, requires less water, and helps preserve the unique Fynbos ecosystem that makes this area a global biodiversity hotspot.

Local Sourcing

Our breakfast ingredients come from local farms and our own gardens when possible. Building materials were sourced regionally, reducing transportation emissions. Artworks, furniture, and decor all come from South African creators, keeping economic benefits within the community.

Waste Reduction

We compost organic waste, recycle diligently, and avoid single-use plastics wherever possible. Our toiletries are eco-friendly and sourced from sustainable producers. Small choices compound into significant impact over time.
